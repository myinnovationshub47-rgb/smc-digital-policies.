

# Privacy Policy — SMC Digital Test Requisition

**Effective date:** July 18, 2026

**Last updated:** July 18, 2026

## 1. Who We Are

This application ("SMC Digital Test Requisition" or "the App") is developed and published by an authorized individual developer acting on behalf of **Silchar Medical College & Hospital (SMCH)**, located at Ghungoor, Silchar, Assam, India — 788014. The App is an internal laboratory information system used exclusively by authorized SMCH medical and administrative staff.

## 2. What Data This App Collects

This App does not collect data from or about patients directly. All patient data processed through this App is manually submitted by SMCH staff in their professional capacity for clinical and operational purposes.

**Data entered by staff users:**

* **Personal account data:** Staff name, username, email address, and assigned role.
* **Patient clinical data:** Patient name, manually entered UHID/Hospital Number (not auto-generated), age, gender, department, contact number, address, provisional diagnosis, medication history, specimen information, and payment receipt details.

**Data generated by app usage:**

* **Session tokens:** Stored locally on the device in encrypted storage and not transmitted to third parties.
* **Offline requisition queue:** Stored locally in encrypted form until successfully synced to the SMCH backend.
* **Audit logs:** Usage and action entries submitted to the SMCH backend for security and accountability.

**Device permissions used:**

* **Camera:** Used exclusively for on-device barcode scanning. No camera images are captured, saved, or transmitted to any server.
* **Internet:** Required to communicate securely with the SMCH backend hosted on Google's infrastructure.

## 3. How Data is Stored and Transmitted

All data submitted through this App is routed to and stored within Google Sheets and Google Drive infrastructure under SMCH's Google Workspace account, housed within Google's secure data centers.

Data in transit is strictly protected by TLS/HTTPS encryption. Session tokens and the offline data queue are stored locally on the user's device utilizing Android's `EncryptedSharedPreferences` and an encrypted Room database.

## 4. Who Data is Shared With

Patient and staff data entered into this App is accessible solely to authorized SMCH personnel based on their strict role assignments. Data is never sold, shared with advertisers, or transmitted to third parties, except as outlined below for operational infrastructure:

* **Google LLC:** Data is stored on Google infrastructure under SMCH's workspace. This processing is governed by Google's Terms of Service and Data Processing Amendment.
* **WhatsApp / Meta Platforms:** If enabled by SMCH, automated SMS or WhatsApp notifications regarding sample statuses (e.g., "Report ready for despatch") may be routed through the WhatsApp Business API via SMCH's Meta Business account.

## 5. Data Retention

Clinical and operational data retained in the SMCH backend is subject to the hospital's internal medical records retention policy. Locally cached offline records on the mobile device are purged automatically upon successful synchronization to the server. Session tokens are destroyed upon user logout or after 6 hours of inactivity.

## 6. Your Rights Under India's DPDPA 2023

As a Data Principal under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you retain the right to access, correct, and request the erasure of your personal data held by SMCH. To exercise these rights, please contact the Data Protection Officer using the details provided in Section 11.

## 7. Security Measures

We implement industry-standard technical safeguards to protect your data. This includes enforced HTTPS for all network traffic, AES-256-GCM encryption for locally stored session credentials, and strict role-based access controls (RBAC) enforced at the backend level. Users remain responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of their login credentials.

## 8. Account Deletion

Google Play requires a clear mechanism for users to request account deletion. To request the deletion of your staff account and associated personal data:

* **In-App:** Navigate to Settings > Account > Request Account Deletion.
* **Via Email:** Send an email to the Data Protection Officer with the subject "Account Deletion Request" and include your registered username.

SMCH will process the request within 30 days, subject to mandatory legal and clinical audit retention obligations.

## 9. Children's Privacy

This App is strictly for professional use by adult SMCH staff. It is not directed at individuals under 18 years of age and does not knowingly collect personal data from minors acting as app users.

## 10. Changes to This Policy

Material changes to this Privacy Policy will be communicated via in-app notifications or to registered staff email addresses at least 14 days before taking effect. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.

We retain your personal account information only for as long as your account is active and you are an authorized affiliate of Silchar Medical College & Hospital (SMCH).

### How to Delete Your Account and Data

*   **In-App Deletion:** You can permanently delete your account and associated login data directly within the App. Navigate to the **Profile Settings** screen and click **"Delete Account & Data"**. This will immediately remove your credentials and profile information from our active database.
*   **Web / Email Deletion Request:** If you no longer have access to the App, you can request account deletion by contacting the system administrator at **[drajit.smc01@gmail.com]**. Please send the email from the address registered to your account with the subject line "Account Deletion Request".

### Data Retained for Legal and Audit Purposes

Please note that because this application handles clinical workflows and medical test requisitions, any clinical records, patient data entries, and system audit logs you generated during your authorized use of the App cannot be deleted upon account termination. This data is strictly retained to comply with hospital governance, clinical audit trails, and Indian healthcare data regulations (including the DPDPA 2023).
